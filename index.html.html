About Me

I’m Lucy Mwaniki — an EdTech specialist, former rugby player and referee, and passionate advocate for inclusive, meaningful learning experiences across Africa.

Who I Am

My journey started in the sciences but quickly expanded into the world of education, tech, and hands-on learning. Before I was building tech tools or managing STEM programs, I was on the rugby pitch — learning teamwork, resilience, and how to stay grounded under pressure. That mindset still drives how I lead projects, collaborate with communities, and design learning tools that actually work.

  • I blend science, tech, and human-centered design
  • I bring the discipline of a ref and the creativity of a maker
  • I care deeply about equity and relevance in education
  • I believe learning should spark both confidence and curiosity

Mission

I want to make education feel possible, practical, and powerful — especially for learners who are often left out of the conversation.

  • Empower teachers with practical tech tools
  • Design inclusive learning experiences for all learners
  • Make AI, robotics, and coding feel local and doable
  • Support youth-led innovation and creativity

Values & Approach

I believe meaningful education starts with how we show up — not just what we teach. My work is guided by empathy, collaboration, and a deep respect for learners and educators alike.

  • Human-centered: I design with people, not just for them
  • Inclusive: I prioritize accessibility and gender equity
  • Playful: I believe learning should be joyful and hands-on
  • Reflective: I’m always learning, adapting, and improving
